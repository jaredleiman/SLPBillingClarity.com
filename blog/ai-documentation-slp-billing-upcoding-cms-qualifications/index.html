AI Documentation and SLP Upcoding: What the BCBS Findings Mean for Independent Speech Pathologists

Blue Cross Blue Shield quantified $2.3 billion in AI-driven hospital upcoding in March 2026. Speech-language pathology wasn't in the BCBS sample, but the specialty is in a uniquely vulnerable position â CMS reversed the 2024 SLP qualification ruling in July 2025, resetting the rules for who can bill what, and the AI documentation tools SLPs rely on haven't caught up.

Why SLP Is Exposed Right Now

Two forces are converging:

First, the CMS qualification reversal. The July 2025 ruling undid changes that had broadened the scope of who could supervise and who could independently bill for SLP services in certain settings. Many SLPs who built billing workflows under the 2024 rules are now out of compliance without realizing it. AI note templates generated in 2024-2025 may still reflect the old assumptions.

Second, the same AI ambient-scribe mechanism BCBS identified. SLP sessions involve measurable therapy activity, structured assessment, and billed units. AI notes that generalize session content can produce documentation that looks compliant but doesn't match the billed codes.

Where AI Drift Shows Up in SLP

  • 92507 vs 92526. Treatment of speech, language, voice, communication disorders (92507) versus treatment of swallowing disorders and oral function (92526). Both can be appropriate â but AI notes that default to 92526 because it reimburses slightly higher in some fee schedules drive outlier patterns.
  • 92610 clinical swallowing evaluation documentation. Specific elements required â oral-motor exam, food trials, clinical observations. AI notes that generalize "swallow assessment performed" without specific elements fail medical review.
  • 92523 / 92524 evaluations. Time-based; AI notes that overstate evaluation time produce audit flags.
  • Unit inflation on untimed codes. SLP has a mix of timed and untimed codes. Confusion between the two, amplified by AI scribes that don't distinguish them clearly, drives over-billing.
  • GN modifier attestation. Required on all SLP claims. Some scribe-integrated billing workflows drop it on certain claim types.

What the 2025 Qualification Reversal Added

SLPs who supervised or independently billed under the 2024 rules in specific Part B outpatient settings now need to verify their current status matches CMS's reversed framework. This isn't about AI â but AI-generated note templates that reference "supervising SLP-A" language from 2024 can misrepresent service delivery in ways that create overpayment exposure retroactively.

Your MAC already has your 2024 and 2025 billing patterns. If the pattern shifted in a way that the reversal now makes non-compliant, the exposure is real.

  1. Pull five recent 92507 encounters and five 92526 encounters.
  2. For each 92526, verify the note documents swallowing/oral-function-specific work, not mixed speech-and-swallow.

  4. Check timed-code billing against documented face-to-face time.
  5. Confirm GN modifier on every claim.
